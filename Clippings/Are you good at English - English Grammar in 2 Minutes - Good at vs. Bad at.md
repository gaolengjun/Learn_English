### Intro

**0:05** · This is English Grammar in 2 Minutes.

**0:08** · When we are talking about our skills, talents, strengths, or weaknesses, we can use “good at + noun/gerund” or “bad at “noun/gerund”.

### Good at

**0:22** · So, it is very simple. Look at these examples.

**0:26** · He is good at soccer.

**0:30** · She is good at math.

**0:34** · I am good at cooking.

**0:37** · They are good at playing guitar.

**0:44** · You can also make sentences with words that are similar to “good/bad”.

**0:54** · Look at these examples.

**0:57** · She is okay at playing golf.

**1:01** · He is great at science.

**1:04** · I am terrible at singing.

**1:08** · They are horrible at sports.

### Good with

**1:18** · Change “at” to “with” when you are talking about people, tools, money, or numbers.

**1:28** · These are things that you use or deal with.

**1:35** · She is good with money.

**1:37** · (She is good at handling money) He is good with computers.

**1:45** · (He is good at using computers.)

**1:51** · He is good with children.

**1:53** · (He is good at dealing with children.)

**2:00** · They are good with numbers.

**2:02** · (They are good at dealing with numbers.)
